Pradhan Mantri Kisan Maandhan Yojana

Launched on 12th September 2019, this scheme provides social security to Small and Marginal Farmers in their old age when they have minimal or no savings.

This pension scheme is specifically designed for farmers who own less than 2 hectares of land. Farmers between the ages of 18 to 40 years are eligible to apply. They must contribute a monthly premium between ₹55 to ₹200, depending on their age, until they reach the age of 60. Upon reaching 60 years of age, they will receive a monthly pension of ₹3,000. The scheme also includes provisions for the spouse of the farmer to receive a pension in case of the farmer’s demise, making it a vital safety net for the farming community.

Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana

Launched on 18th February 2016, this scheme aims to provide financial assistance to farmers in case of crop loss due to natural calamities, pests, and diseases.

The Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) offers crop insurance to farmers at highly subsidized rates. It covers all food crops, oilseeds, and annual commercial/horticultural crops. The premium for kharif crops is 2%, for rabi crops is 1.5%, and for commercial/horticultural crops, it is 5% of the sum insured. The central and state governments share the premium burden to ensure farmers pay a nominal rate. Claims are paid based on the loss assessment conducted by government officials, ensuring that farmers are compensated for their losses and can continue farming activities without financial distress.

PM-Kisan Samman Nidhi

Under this scheme, farmer families with cultivable land of up to 2 hectares receive annual income support of ₹6,000 in three equal installments.

The PM-Kisan scheme is a direct benefit transfer initiative aimed at ensuring financial support to small and marginal farmers. The scheme is fully funded by the Government of India and provides income support to approximately 14 crore farmer families. The ₹6,000 annual support is transferred directly to the bank accounts of the farmers in three installments of ₹2,000 each. This financial assistance helps farmers meet their input costs and other expenses, thereby reducing their dependence on moneylenders. The scheme also focuses on promoting better agricultural practices and enhancing farm productivity.

Pashudhan Bima Yojana

This scheme offers insurance for livestock, providing compensation in case of accidental death or disease, helping farmers manage risks effectively.

Pashudhan Bima Yojana is a livestock insurance scheme designed to protect farmers against the loss of their animals, which are a critical source of livelihood. The scheme covers cattle, buffaloes, sheep, goats, and other livestock. Farmers pay a nominal premium, and the insurance covers accidental death, permanent disability, diseases, and other risks. The compensation is based on the market value of the animal at the time of the claim. The scheme aims to ensure that farmers do not face financial ruin due to the loss of their livestock and can continue their farming activities without interruption.

Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchan Yojana

This national mission aims to enhance agricultural productivity by ensuring the availability of water to farms through efficient use of resources.

The Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchan Yojana (PMKSY) focuses on improving water-use efficiency in the agricultural sector. The scheme integrates various components like Accelerated Irrigation Benefit Programme (AIBP), Har Khet Ko Pani (Water for Every Field), Per Drop More Crop, and Watershed Development. PMKSY aims to expand cultivable land under irrigation, improve on-farm water use efficiency to reduce wastage, and enhance the adoption of precision irrigation techniques like drip and sprinkler systems. By ensuring water availability for farming, PMKSY aims to double farmers’ incomes and promote sustainable agricultural practices across the country.
